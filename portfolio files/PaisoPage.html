Paiso

UI/UX design.

Project Overview

Paiso is an app for splitting expenses between you and your friends. It lets users add bills and keep track of who owes whom, and consequnetly helps settle up expanses with each other. Aditionally, the app allows users to track balances, organize expanses quickly on the go!

My role

As a team, we performed user research and competitive analysis and created user flow, wireframes, and prototype in a 6-week spring. I worked on this real client project as part of my Academy UX/UX Design course.

Design process

I used a 5-stage Design Thinking model proposed by theHasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford (the "d. school"). The stages of this Design Thinking process are as follows:
1. Empathise, 2. Define, 3. Ideate, 4. Prototype, 5. Test.

Design Toolkit

Home

We introduced the homepage in order to provide a central place for a user to go from when starting their Paiso journey.

The home page displays some of the essential function of the app that includes, the total outstanding balance, how much you, or your friends owe you, and option to switch between different groups.

Add expanse

This is where users add their expanse by selecting either a group or individuals. After making the selection, users are then required to enter the description, details regarding the bill.

Additionally, users can also add photos of the recipient and even notes to add more details. After entering all the details, users can press save, and the transaction will be recorder over.

Group View

This particular page houses all the activities from different groups that users are involved in. Along with the recent transactions, users have options to check their total outstanding balance from the group.
The page also includes options to settle up the expanses, check balances and totals in more detailed manner. An additional feature of the app allows to see the latest trends with the help graphs, and consequently compare between them.

User Research

Research strategies

The first thing we focused on was our research strategy. In order to identify the flaws in the current UX and UI, we conducted a usability test by interviewing people that fit our user archetype. These were users between the age of 18 to 25 years who share expanses with friends and roommates. Before we began our interviews we first needed to do few things.
First we conducted a competitor analysis. Aside from using and understanding Paiso, this also gave us an insight in the market space and provided additional context for the problem we are working on. Secondly we created an interview guide in preparation for usability testing. This enabled us to conduct our interviews in a consistent and controlled manner

Competitors analysis

As a part of our competitor Analysis, we conducted an analysis on two apps with similar offerings to Paiso.
Those were, SPLID, and Settle UP. We focused our research on user interface elements and user experience flows,including the sign up process, recording stage, user ratings and features.

Competitors color scheme

Both of the apps more or less carry the same theme across the pages, a white background, with use of grey and dominating orange in options and menus. Significance of using orange grey and white is that they represent a clean, minimal look with the warmth of orange, giving an uninterrupted and diversion free experience to the user. Additionally, psychological evidence suggests that orange creates a feeling of excitement, joy, and freshness which closely relates to our targeted audience.

Competitors sign-up process

These are the different ways in which the different apps have approached their Sign-Up process. With Splid new users have options to either create a group or join a pre-existing group, without creating any account. Users are then required to add new group members, invite them, and start adding expanses.

Moving on to settle up, users have few options to create new account, either with apple, google, Facebook or with existing email. After the login, users are then required to create or join a nearby group to start using the app.

Competitors: adding expanse

The adding expans function for Splid indicates the user to record their expanses using the plus button on the bottom which opens up three options namely new expanse, new payment, and new person. With the help of the included options and clear instructions, users are able to quickly add record new expanses, make payments and add a new person to the group whom they want to share the expanses with. Moving on, clicking on new expanses takes users on a new page which displays the name of the expanse, the amount and split information. Aditionally, users have an option to edit and make their changes accordingly. After clicking save, users can see the updated changes on the home screen.

When it comes to Settle-Up, users have slightly more complex interface to deal with compared to split. In order to add expanses, users are supposed to press the plus floating icon, which then takes them to another page, which displays the amount carried forward, the user who is paying for it, with the included option for changing currencies, which is very helpful when travelling internationally. By clicking next, users can record the expanses which are then displayed on its dashboard.

Research objectives

The intent of the UX usability testing activity is to identify any usability issues including:

  • Assess whether the terminology is understood by users and appropriate language is used.
  • Determine whether the process flows in a logical way.
  • Assess if users can effectively navigate through the process using the UI elements.
  • Assess whether the content adequately supports users.
  • Analysis of how the design measures up against user goals, business goals, project intent, and system or technical constraints

Research participant demographic

For our usability testing we interviewed 3 different people: Hans, James, and John. The uses were located in australia, germany and canada. There was a range of technical abilities

Research synthesis

After our interview we grouped and categorized the data into an Affinity Map which enabled us to have a clearer understanding of the user experience.

Consequently, this allowed us to create an Empathy Map which enables us to step into the shoes of the user.

User Flow

User on boarding.

On boarding section

  • The intro text made sense. It was clear and simple to read.
  • However, throughout the app, users felt there was too much text to read. This meant that some information was not being read which led to confusion.
  • Users felt the design was too formal and the initial impression was not welcoming.
  • The process of logging the expanses/selecting the users and saving them were not clear enough
  • The users thought that app automatically split the expanses between the users and saved it.
  • Users were unable to create new users while recording a new transaction and had to abandon the whole process/go back to home screen and create the new users

Design System

We came up with a design system to add consistency, structure, and communication across all team members. We chose simple readable humanist typeface that would improve reading legibility at both small and large sizes. The color palette is mature yet modern and has good contrast. We also implemented icons and friendly illustrations to bring some fun and enjoyment to the app.

The prototype

We have combined all the elements shown throughout the design process to create what we feel is a warm, functional, fun and approachable app.
